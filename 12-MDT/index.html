A keyboard is one of the primary input devices used with a computer.
Being able to use a keyboard comfortably can make a big difference in your comfort level and productivity.
When you're buying a new keyboard, it's important to make sure that it will meet your needs.
Choosing a good keyboard can have a big impact on your typing speed, acuaracy, and overall comfort while typing, but most imporant on your health; because a bad keyboard can cause you a lot of pain in your hands, wrists, and arms.

There are many factors to consider when choosing a keyboard, such as the size, durability, and software support and layout.

Size

The size of the keyboard refers to the number of keys and additional features like a numeric keypad, media keys, and more.

Durability

The durability of the keyboard refers to how long it will last before the keys stop working or the keyboard breaks, ther are many options

Software support

The software support of the keyboard refers to the ability to customize the keyboard, most common being RGB lighting and macro keys.

Layout

The layout of the keyboard refers to the way the keys are arranged. Is the most important factor to consider when buying a keyboard, because it will determine how comfortable you are while typing.

What it is? A staggered keyboard is typical for many keyboards, including your built-in laptop keyboard. It comes from the design of the vintage typewriter wherein each key must avoid bumping into one another for smooth registration.
PROS CONS
  • It is the most common layout
  • It is easy to type on as you are already used to its layout.
  • There is no learning curve should you decide to acquire a staggered external keyboard.
  • The market is also much more saturated.
  • more customization options are available when using a staggered keyboard. Keyboard plates and foams come pre-cut to fit staggered keyboards more often than not.
  • Most of the case's they are cheaper.
  • Staggered is not the most ergonomic layout.
  • It is not the most efficient layout. You will experience more finger travel and finger fatigue when typing on a staggered.
  • Staggered keyborad is bigger than ortholinear, making it less portable and taking more desktop space.
  • There is no reason that the keyboard layout should still be staggered.
  • Staggered layout keyboards may not be as unique and novelty as ortholinear keyboards
  • Staggered keyboard are difficult to repair.
What it is? An ortholinear keyboard is a keyboard that aligns in a columnar manner. This means that the keys are in perfect rows, up and down and left to right. This layout minimizes finger movement from key to key and is ideal for professional typists who endure long hours of practice.
PROS CONS
  • The are smaller, meaning they are more portable and take less desktop space.
  • It minimizes finger movement from key to key and is ideal for professional typists who endure long hours of practice.
  • It is more ergonomic, because it allows you to keep your hands in a more natural position.
  • Usually they are programmable, meaning you can customize the layout to your needs or what you like.
  • They are ←doit yourself→ friendly, meaning you can build your own keyboard, meaning you can have "unlimited" customization.
  • It is easyer to repair, because most of the pieces are hot swapable and you can buy them separately.
  • There is a learnig curve to get use to type on.
  • Probably you will need to learn tap dancing.
  • The market is less saturated, making it sometimes difficult to find what you want.
  • Becaus is ←doit yourself→ usually you will need to learn about keyboard pieces and in the worst case electronics and soldering. (Ther almost no prebuilt ortholinear keyboards on the market)
  • Most of the time Ortholinear are more expensive.
